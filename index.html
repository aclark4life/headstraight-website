October, 2004 Vol. 21, No. 10 Issue #241

Grab Bag

By Greg Yost

Power pop is a style of music that doesn't seem very prevalent in the Baltimore and Washington, D.C. region, but I discovered two local bands working hard to fill that void. Both Ape House and Headstraight are carrying the power pop torch in the region with their great music...

...Baltimore's Headstraight has a slightly darker sound than Ape House, but the power pop formula is still evident. The band just put out an EP called Buried to showcase its sound and the three tracks are very impressive. Of particular note is the grungy "Not Enough." This song sounds like it was pulled from Soundgarden's material from the early 90's.... Both bands are making great music and are worth checking out....

NEXT DOOR CULTURE
NEIGHBORHOOD PERFORMANCE
Alex Clark of McKinley Street founded local band Headstraight

Being in a band might seem glamorous, but according to Alex Clark, it's a lot of hard work. Not only is Alex the founding member of Headstraight, he's also the band's lead vocalist, songwriter and publicist. Alex always loved music, but he didn't start playing guitar until high school. He cites the band Nirvana as a major influence. "I wanted to travel and tour the country with my music, like they did," he says. When he graduated college with a computer science degree in 1998, Alex decided it was time to give his dream a try. After 3 years of ups and downs in Baltimore, Alex moved to Bethesda, where Headstraight was eventually formed. Their music is a mix of rock and pop, but Alex categorizes the band as having an Alternative Rock sound. Currently, Alex freelances developing software, which allows him to concentrate his efforts on the band's future. "I feel like we're making progress and building momentum," he says. Headstraight's first album is due out the end of July, and the band will promote its release by broadcasting to 300 college radio stations. You can catch Headstraight on July 11 at TT Reynolds in Fairfax, VA.
